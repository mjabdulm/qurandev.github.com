The Sky

سَمٓاء
- Essentially means height without limitation.
- Purely linguistically, anything that is above another thing is a سَمٓاء and that which is below is أرض. This explains the usage in ayah 65:12
- 65:12  ٱللَّهُ ٱلَّذِى خَلَقَ سَبْعَ سَمَٰوَٰتٍ وَمِنَ ٱلْأَرْضِ مِثْلَهُنَّ  
- Where the sky is used for heights above, the word أرض is used for depths below. 7:176
- 7:176  وَلَوْ شِئْنَا لَرَفَعْنَٰهُ بِهَا وَلَٰكِنَّهُۥٓ أَخْلَدَ إِلَى ٱلْأَرْضِ  
فلك
- Though commonly interpreted as the sky, it actually refers to the paths or orbits assigned to planets and stars
- These orbits are usually oval in shape and so the word is appropriate in that they resemble the shape of a ship الفُلْك !
- 36:40  وَكُلٌّ فِى فَلَكٍ يَسْبَحُونَ
